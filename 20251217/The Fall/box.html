Armenia

The Color of Pomegranates (1969) is a visually stunning, non-linear Armenian film by Sergei Parajanov, depicting the life of poet Sayat-Nova through symbolic, painterly tableaus rather than traditional narrative, rich with Armenian culture, folklore, and religious imagery, especially featuring the pomegranate's symbolic reds and golds as metaphors for life, love, and spirit, making it a landmark of poetic cinema.
